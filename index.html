Eligibility Criteria — Are You Eligible?

To appear for JEE Advanced 2026, you must fulfill all of the following conditions:

  • JEE Main Performance: Rank among the top 2,50,000 qualified candidates in JEE Main 2026.
  • Age Limit: Born on or after October 1, 2001 (General). SC/ST/PwD candidates get a 5-year relaxation.
  • Attempt Limit: Maximum of two consecutive attempts at JEE Advanced.
  • Class 12 Board Qualification: Passed Class 12 (or equivalent) in 2025 or 2026 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • No Prior IIT Admission: Must not have previously accepted an IIT seat through JoSAA.
📋

Exam Pattern — What to Expect

JEE Advanced tests depth of understanding across Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. Here is what the exam looks like:

  • Two compulsory papers (Paper 1 & Paper 2), each of 3 hours duration.
  • Subjects: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics covered in each paper.
  • Question types include MCQs (single and multiple correct answers), numerical value-based questions, and matching-type questions.
  • Question count and marking scheme may vary as decided by the organizing IIT each year.
  • Negative marking applies — precision and accuracy are critical. Quality over quantity!

ACAD's Top 6 Preparation Tips

With the exam on 17 May 2026, every week counts. Here are our expert-backed strategies to help you crack JEE Advanced:

01

Master NCERT First, Then Go Deeper

NCERT builds your JEE Main foundation. For Advanced, go beyond with H.C. Verma (Physics), O.P. Tandon (Chemistry), and S.L. Loney (Maths).

02

Solve Previous Year Papers

Work through at least 10 years of JEE Advanced papers under timed conditions. Analyze every mistake — understand the "why" behind each answer.

03

Build Conceptual Clarity

JEE Advanced rewards deep understanding over rote memorization. Spend time on derivations and real-world applications, not just formulae.

04

Weekly Full-Length Mock Tests

Regular mock tests build time management and exam temperament. Review your performance weekly with your ACAD mentor to plug weak areas.

05

Balance All Three Subjects

A weak subject can cost you your IIT dream. Track your weekly progress across Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics without neglecting any.

06

Prioritize Health & Rest

8 hours of sleep, regular exercise, and short study breaks are non-negotiable. A healthy mind retains and applies knowledge far better.
